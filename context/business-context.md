# Business Context

This is the qualitative context the AI step uses to explain **why** a change
matters — the kind of org knowledge a real deployment would draw from Claude
Enterprise or hand-written context. It contains **no numbers**: the CRM owns
every figure. This file only explains what the accounts are and how to read a
change strategically.

## Who we are

We're an alliance & partnerships team. The "pipeline" is our book of strategic
alliance opportunities across the sectors we operate in — energy, logistics,
healthcare, public sector, mobility, manufacturing, and financial services. Each
opportunity is a partnership we're working to land or expand. We report progress
on an internal site so leadership and sector leads can see where things stand.

Closed-won for the period is our headline number: it's the partnership value
we've actually booked against our target. Being ahead or behind goal is the
first thing anyone looks at.

## How to read a change

- **Amount up at an open stage** → the partner expanded scope or committed to
  more. Often a leading signal of a renewal or an upsell landing soon.
- **Amount down** → scope was trimmed or the deal was renegotiated. Worth a flag.
- **Stage → Closed Won** → booked. It moves the closed-won number and the goal.
- **Stage → Closed Lost** → the pursuit ended; a prompt to revisit our position
  in that account or sector.
- **New opportunity** → a fresh partnership entered the pipeline.

When you write the "why," reason from the account's role below plus the kind of
change above. Keep it to one or two confident sentences. Make it plausible and
specific — never invent a figure, and never overstate certainty about cause
(say what a move "typically signals," not what definitely happened).
